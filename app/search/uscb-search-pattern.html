Description

Purpose

Search widgets are means for users to look for content without manually clicking through the web page.

Usage

Search widgets can take on a variety of aesthetic qualities, but they essentially serve similar purposes: to find content without having to dive into web pages using many clicks. These widgets are most commonly known as "Search Bars," and include an area for text input and a call to action that initiates the search. Search bars in this platform also include predictive text, which completes users' search terms using character matching algorithms. The magnifying glass icon that indicates the call to action for initiating the query.

Occurrences: Single Search bar page, Results Search (Searches all of CEDSCI's data to find matching content), Filter side panel (Search bar under Topic, Geographies, and Industries), and the Maps tool ("Find State, County, City, etc.").

Do
  • Provide predictive text to help users find what they need.
  • Differentiate different kinds of queries in the predictive texts by section (tables, keywords, etc.).
  • Take advantage of both global search bar patterns and local search bar patterns.
Do not
  • Overcomplicate the search bar.
  • Make too many search bars.
